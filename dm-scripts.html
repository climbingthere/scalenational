First Touch — Pick One
Send only one opener. Test all of them over time and track reply rates.
Opener · Curiosity Gap
"Hey [Name] — I have a list of outdoor brands we're actively looking to place UGC creators with for paid ads. Wanted to see if [Brand] would be a fit before I reach out to someone else in the space."
Why this works: Creates urgency + scarcity without being salesy. The "someone else" line implies competition and makes them feel like an opportunity — not a target.
Opener · Audit Play
"Hey [Name] — took a look at [Brand]'s current Meta ads. I have a few ideas that could move your ROAS pretty quickly. Would it be weird if I broke them down for you?"
Why this works: Shows you did the work before asking for anything. "Would it be weird" is a pattern interrupt — it lowers resistance and gets a yes.
Opener · Competitor Intel
"Hey [Name] — noticed one of your main competitors is killing it with UGC right now on Meta. Wanted to reach out before the window closes — are you running paid ads at all right now?"
Why this works: Loss aversion. Nobody wants to watch a competitor win. Ends with a low-commitment yes/no question.
Opener · Pattern Interrupt
"This is either perfectly timed or completely irrelevant — is [Brand] actively scaling paid ads right now?"
Why this works: Disarms them immediately by acknowledging it might not be relevant. Honest framing builds trust fast.
Opener · Single Question
"Do you have someone running paid ads for [Brand] right now?"
Why this works: The simplest opener that exists. One question, no pitch, no pressure. Works because it asks for information not commitment.
Opener · Creator Angle
"Hey [Name] — I run @kirtoutdoors (50M+ views in the outdoor space) and also run a performance ad agency that places UGC creators in Meta campaigns. [Brand] caught my eye — do you have a minute to hear what we've been doing?"
Why this works: Social proof from the creator side establishes instant credibility. You're not a random agency — you're someone who already has proven reach in their exact niche.
Full Outreach Flows
Choose a play. Send message 1. Wait for reply. Move to next message only if no response.
Audit Play — 4-Touch Sequence
Day 1 — Opener
"Hey [Name] — took a look at [Brand]'s current Meta ads. I have a few ideas that could move your ROAS pretty quickly. Would it be weird if I broke them down for you?"
Wait 48 hrs — No reply? Send →
Day 3 — Follow Up
"Wanted to bump this up — I only put together audits for brands I think have real upside. [Brand] has it. 10 minutes on a call would be enough to give you something useful."
Wait 48 hrs — No reply? Send →
Day 5 — Social Proof
"Just wrapped a similar campaign for an outdoor gear brand — got them from 1.8x to 4.1x ROAS in 6 weeks. Thought of you. Still happy to share what we found in the audit."
Wait 72 hrs — No reply? Send →
Day 8 — The Close
"Last message from me — I don't want to be that person. If the timing is off or it's not a fit, totally get it. But if you ever want to see what we found, the audit's ready whenever you are."
Why this works: The final message removes all pressure. Paradoxically this gets the most replies because it signals you respect their time and won't chase.

They've Engaged Before
Someone who liked a post, watched a reel, visited your page, or you have a mutual connection with.
Warm · Mutual Connection
"Hey [Name] — noticed we're both connected with [Mutual]. Small world. I run a performance ad agency for outdoor brands — saw [Brand] and thought there might be something worth talking about. Are you the right person for that conversation?"
Why this works: Borrowed trust. Mutual connections create instant credibility without you having to earn it.
Warm · Content Engagement
"Hey [Name] — you liked one of our posts a while back. Not sure if you looked into what we do, but we specifically work with outdoor brands on paid ads and UGC. Curious if it's something you've been thinking about?"
Why this works: Low-pressure, transparent, and complimentary. It references the action they took without making it awkward.
Warm · Mutual Follow
"Hey [Name] — been following [Brand] for a bit, love what you're building. We work with outdoor brands on Meta campaigns — wondering if paid ads are on your roadmap at all this year?"
Why this works: Genuine compliment first, no hard ask, ends on a soft question about intent.
Handling Responses
They replied — here's exactly what to say to each type of response.
Reply · Who Is This
"Good question — I should've led with more context. I'm Eric, I run ScaleNational — we work with outdoor brands specifically on Meta ads and UGC creator content. Saw [Brand] and thought we might be a fit. What does your paid ad situation look like right now?"
Why this works: Doesn't get defensive. Acknowledges the fair question, gives context fast, ends with a redirect.
Reply · What Is This About
"Short version — we run paid Meta campaigns for outdoor brands and supply the creative (UGC creators, AI video, static ads). Everything done for you. I reached out because [Brand] looks like a brand we could actually move the needle for. Worth a 10-minute call to see if it makes sense?"
Why this works: Clear, fast, no fluff. Ends with a low-commitment ask — a call, not a sale.
Reply · Not Interested
"Totally fair — I appreciate the quick reply. Can I ask one thing? Is it the timing, the service, or something else? Just helps me understand so I don't bother you with the wrong thing in the future."
Why this works: Doesn't close the conversation. Asking why turns a no into information. Often leads to a "actually the timing isn't great, follow up in Q2" which reopens the door.
Reply · Already Have Someone
"Makes sense — good for you for having it sorted. Out of curiosity, are you happy with how it's going? We get a lot of inbounds from brands who have an agency but feel like they're not seeing the ROAS they expected."
Why this works: Doesn't concede. Plants a seed of doubt without being aggressive. If they're secretly unhappy, this opens the door.
Reply · Send Info
"Happy to — easier if I can just ask you two questions first so I send you the right thing: What platforms are you running ads on right now, and roughly what's your monthly ad spend?"
Why this works: Never send a generic deck to a "send me info" request. Qualify first. This also moves the conversation forward and builds rapport.
Reply · They're Interested
"Perfect — let's keep it simple. I have a 20-minute slot [DAY] at [TIME] or [DAY] at [TIME] — does either work? I'll send a quick overview beforehand so we're not starting from zero."
Why this works: Move fast. Don't let interest cool. Two specific times makes it easy to say yes. Sending prep material increases show-up rate.
Pre-Call Priming
Send these after they book a call. Increases show-up rate and warms them up before you pitch.
Immediately After Booking
"Confirmed — talk [DAY] at [TIME]. I'll pull together a quick look at what's working in your space right now so we're not wasting time on basics. Looking forward to it."
Why this works: Sets expectations, signals prep and professionalism, makes them feel like they made the right call.
Day Before Call
"Quick reminder — we're on for tomorrow at [TIME]. I looked at a couple outdoor brands running similar audiences to [Brand] right now. Should have some useful stuff to share. See you then."
Why this works: Reminds them, increases show-up rate, and builds anticipation by teasing value they'll get on the call.
1 Hour Before Call
"Headed into our call in about an hour — here's the link if you need it: [LINK]. Just a heads up, I'll be sharing my screen to walk through a few things — takes about 15 minutes then we can talk through your situation."
Why this works: Reduces no-shows. Framing the call as quick (15 min) lowers their anxiety even if it ends up going longer.
No-Show · Same Day
"Hey [Name] — looks like we missed each other. Totally happens — want to find another time? I'll keep the notes ready."
Why this works: Zero aggression. Keeps the door open without making them feel guilty, which would make them less likely to rebook.

Pre-Send Checklist
Run through this before every outreach. The difference between a reply and a block.
  • Have I personalized the brand name and founder's name?
  • Is this the right person — founder, CMO, or marketing lead?
  • Have I actually looked at their ads / content before sending?
  • Am I sending during business hours (9am–6pm their timezone)?
  • Is my profile / page looking credible if they check it?
  • Did I pick the right opener for this specific lead?
  • Is the message under 3 sentences or clearly structured?
  • Have I removed any generic language or filler words?
  • Does it end with a clear, easy-to-answer question?
The 7 Psychological Principles
1

Curiosity Gap

Open a loop the brain needs to close. Don't give everything upfront.

2

Yes Ladder

Start with easy yes questions before asking for anything real.

3

Loss Aversion

People act to avoid loss 2x more than to seek gain. Use it.

4

Pattern Interrupt

Break their mental script in the first line.

5

Social Proof

Results and names they recognize do your selling for you.

6

Reciprocity

Give first (audit, insight, tip) before you ask for anything.

7

Scarcity / Urgency

Real scarcity ("reaching out to someone else") drives action.
