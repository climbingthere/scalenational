Ad Performance Metrics
Return on Ad Spend
ROAS
Revenue generated for every dollar spent on ads. Formula: Revenue ÷ Ad Spend. A 4x ROAS means every $1 in ad spend returns $4 in revenue.
How to say it "Right now your ROAS is sitting around 1.8x — we want to get you to a consistent 3–4x within 60 days."
Cost Per Acquisition
CPA / CAC
How much it costs to get one paying customer. If you spend $500 and get 10 customers, your CPA is $50.
How to say it "What's your current CAC? If we know your LTV we can figure out exactly how much we can afford to spend per acquisition."
Cost Per Mille
CPM
Cost per 1,000 impressions — what you pay for 1,000 people to see your ad. Lower CPM = cheaper reach.
How to say it "Your CPM is high right now — that tells me the creative or audience isn't resonating. Better creative directly lowers your CPM."
Cost Per Click
CPC
The cost each time someone clicks your ad. A lower CPC means you're sending more traffic to your site for the same budget.
How to say it "Your CPC is $3.80 — industry benchmark for your niche is closer to $1.50. Stronger creative will bring that down significantly."
Click-Through Rate
CTR
Percentage of people who saw the ad and clicked it. Formula: Clicks ÷ Impressions × 100. Higher CTR = more compelling ad.
How to say it "A 1% CTR on cold traffic is solid — if we can get that to 2–3% with better creative, your whole funnel gets cheaper."
Conversion Rate
CVR / CR
Percentage of visitors who take the desired action — purchase, sign up, book a call, etc. Traffic means nothing if CVR is broken.
How to say it "Your ads are getting clicks but the CVR on your landing page is under 1% — the bottleneck isn't the ads, it's the page."
Lifetime Value
LTV / CLV
Total revenue a customer brings over their entire relationship with the brand — not just the first purchase.
How to say it "If your LTV is $400, you can actually afford a $60–80 CAC and still profit. That's why knowing your LTV changes everything."
Marketing Efficiency Ratio
MER
Total revenue divided by total marketing spend across all channels. The big-picture ROAS — it captures everything, not just one campaign.
How to say it "Platform ROAS looks great but what's your MER? If blended revenue vs. total spend is only 1.5x, we have a profitability problem."
Hook Rate
Percentage of people who watch past the first 3 seconds of a video ad. High hook rate = your opener is grabbing attention.
How to say it "Your hook rate is 18% — we want 30%+. The first frame of your video needs to stop the scroll immediately."
Frequency
Average number of times one person sees your ad. Too high causes ad fatigue — performance drops as people stop engaging.
How to say it "Your frequency is at 6.2 — your audience has seen this ad six times. We need fresh creative before performance craters further."
Reach
Number of unique people who saw your ad. Different from impressions — one person seeing your ad five times is 5 impressions but only 1 reach.
How to say it "We reached 42,000 unique people this month — but impressions were 180,000, which means frequency is high. Time to expand the audience."
Impressions
Total number of times your ad was displayed — the same person can count multiple times. High impressions + low reach = high frequency.
How to say it "We got 200,000 impressions — but the real question is how many unique people that represents and whether they converted."
Campaign Structure
Campaign
The top level of a Meta ads structure. Contains ad sets and defines the objective — traffic, conversions, leads, awareness, etc.
How to say it "We'll run two campaigns — one optimizing for purchases, one for retargeting — and let Meta's algorithm allocate spend to what's working."
Ad Set
Middle level of a Meta campaign. Defines the audience, budget, schedule, and placement. Multiple ad sets can run within one campaign.
How to say it "We'll test three ad sets — one broad, one lookalike, one interest-based — and cut anything that doesn't hit our CPA target within a week."
Ad Creative
The actual ad — the image or video, body copy, headline, and CTA button. Creative is the single biggest lever for ad performance.
How to say it "Targeting has gotten harder post-iOS — creative is now the targeting. The right creative self-selects the right audience."
A/B Test
Split Test
Running two versions of an ad simultaneously, changing one variable at a time, to determine which performs better with statistical confidence.
How to say it "We'll A/B test two hooks — same offer, different opening line — so we know exactly what's driving results, not just guessing."
Ad Fatigue
When an audience has seen your ad so many times that engagement drops. Frequency climbs, CTR falls, and costs increase. Needs fresh creative.
How to say it "Your ROAS dropped 40% in two weeks — that's textbook ad fatigue. We need 3–5 new creatives in rotation every month to avoid this."
Audience & Targeting
Cold Traffic
People who have never heard of your brand. Most expensive to convert — they need awareness and trust before they'll buy.
How to say it "For cold traffic we lead with education and social proof — you can't hit a stranger with a hard sell. Warm them up first."
Warm Traffic
People who've already interacted with your brand — visited the site, watched a video, or engaged with content. They convert at a lower CPA.
How to say it "Warm audiences are your lowest-hanging fruit. We should always be retargeting them with a specific offer before spending more on cold."
Retargeting
Remarketing
Showing ads specifically to people who already visited your site or engaged with your content. The highest-converting audience segment.
How to say it "If you don't have a retargeting campaign running, you're basically letting people walk out the door without ever following up."
Lookalike Audience
Meta finds new people who share characteristics with your existing customers or site visitors. A powerful way to scale cold prospecting.
How to say it "Once we have 500+ purchase events, we build a 1% lookalike of your buyers — those are your best-chance cold prospects."
Custom Audience
A specific audience you build from your own data — email lists, website visitors, video viewers, or customer uploads matched to Meta profiles.
How to say it "Do you have an email list? We can upload it as a custom audience and target your existing customers with an upsell before we even touch cold traffic."
Interest Targeting
Targeting people based on their declared interests and behaviors on Facebook/Instagram. Useful for initial testing, less precise than lookalikes.
How to say it "We'll start with interest targeting to gather initial data, but the goal is to build enough signals for lookalikes — that's where the real scale is."
Funnel
Funnel
The journey a customer takes from first seeing your brand to making a purchase. Each stage needs different messaging and ad creative.
How to say it "We build a full-funnel approach — awareness at the top, education in the middle, and a hard offer at the bottom for people ready to buy."
Top of Funnel
TOFU
Awareness stage. People who don't know your brand yet. Goal is to generate interest and build an audience to retarget later.
How to say it "TOFU is where we build the pipeline — we're not expecting sales here, we're buying attention and building the audience we'll close later."
Middle of Funnel
MOFU
Consideration stage. People who've shown interest but haven't bought. Nurture them with social proof, comparisons, testimonials, and demos.
How to say it "MOFU is where we overcome objections — testimonials, before/afters, FAQs. We're removing every reason not to buy."
Bottom of Funnel
BOFU
Decision stage. People close to purchasing — they've visited your site or added to cart. Retargeting campaigns live here with direct offers.
How to say it "BOFU is where we close. Cart abandoners, product page visitors — these people are one nudge away from converting and they're cheap to reach."
Above the Fold
The part of a webpage visible without scrolling. Critical placement — your headline, hero image, and social proof need to land here.
How to say it "Your value prop needs to hit above the fold — if a visitor has to scroll to understand what you do, you're losing them before you start."
Landing Page
The specific page someone lands on after clicking your ad. Should be focused on one conversion goal — not the homepage with 10 distractions.
How to say it "Sending ad traffic to your homepage is leaving money on the table. We build dedicated landing pages matched to each offer and audience."
Creative Types
User Generated Content
UGC
Content created by real people, not brands. Looks authentic, feels organic, and blends into the feed — which is why it converts so well in paid ads.
How to say it "UGC performs so well because it doesn't look like an ad. It's someone talking about your product like a friend would — that trust is impossible to fake with polished creative."
Static Ad
A still image ad. Simple and fast to produce — and often outperforms video in cost-per-purchase for direct-response campaigns.
How to say it "Don't underestimate statics — a well-designed image with a bold headline and clear offer regularly beats expensive video in direct response."
Video Ad
A moving image ad. Higher production cost but powerful for storytelling, product demos, and hook-based content that builds trust fast.
How to say it "For your product, video is going to work really well — it needs a demo to make the value obvious. We'll pair it with strong statics for retargeting."
Carousel Ad
An ad with multiple swipeable images or cards — great for product ranges, step-by-step processes, or showcasing multiple benefits in one ad.
How to say it "Carousel ads are great for product lines — each card features a different item so one ad can serve multiple customer intents at once."
Social Proof
Reviews, testimonials, follower counts, press mentions — anything that proves other people trust your product. One of the highest-leverage elements in any ad.
How to say it "Your reviews are your best asset — we should be leading with them in every MOFU and BOFU ad. Social proof removes the risk in the buyer's mind."
Hook
The first 1–3 seconds of a video or the opening line of copy. The hook is the single most important element — it determines whether anyone sees the rest.
How to say it "We test hooks more than anything else — because if you don't stop the scroll in the first two seconds, nothing else in the ad matters."
Tracking & Attribution
Meta Pixel
A small piece of code on your website that tracks visitor behavior and reports back to Meta — essential for optimization, lookalikes, and retargeting.
How to say it "Before we spend a dollar, we need your pixel installed and firing correctly. Without it, Meta is flying blind and your campaigns will underperform."
Events API
CAPI
Server-side version of the pixel. More reliable tracking that works around iOS 14+ privacy changes and browser ad blockers.
How to say it "Post-iOS 14, browser pixels miss a lot of conversions. CAPI sends data directly from your server to Meta — you get cleaner data and better optimization."
Attribution Window
The time period Meta uses to credit a sale to an ad. 7-day click means if someone buys within 7 days of clicking, Meta claims credit.
How to say it "Your ROAS looks different on Meta vs. in your Shopify dashboard because of attribution windows — Meta often claims more credit than it actually deserves."
UTM Parameters
Tags added to URLs to track exactly which ad, campaign, or channel drove a visit — readable in Google Analytics or any analytics platform.
How to say it "We tag every ad link with UTMs so you can see in Google Analytics exactly which campaign is driving revenue — platform-agnostic truth."
Business Terms
Key Performance Indicator
KPI
A specific, measurable goal used to track progress. Without defined KPIs, you can't tell if a campaign is succeeding or failing.
How to say it "Before we launch, let's align on KPIs. Is it a target ROAS? A maximum CPA? A revenue goal? We build the whole strategy around that number."
Direct to Consumer
DTC
A brand that sells directly to the end customer, cutting out retailers. DTC brands own their customer data, margins, and experience.
How to say it "You're DTC, which means paid social is your primary growth channel — you don't have shelf space or retail distribution to rely on."
Average Order Value
AOV
The average dollar amount per transaction. Higher AOV means you can afford a higher CPA and still hit your margin targets.
How to say it "What's your AOV? If it's $40 we have very little room on CPA — but if we can bundle or upsell to $90+, the whole math changes."
Churn
The rate at which customers stop buying or cancel their subscription. High churn means your LTV is low and your CPA ceiling is low too.
How to say it "If churn is high, throwing more ad spend at acquisition just papers over the problem. We'd look at retention first — fix the leaky bucket."
Organic
Content or traffic that isn't paid for — comes from followers, SEO, word of mouth, or shares. Complements paid but doesn't replace it at scale.
How to say it "Your organic content is great trust-building, but organic reach on Meta is near zero now. Paid is how you actually get it in front of new people."
Paid Social
Advertising on social media platforms — Meta (Facebook/Instagram), TikTok, Pinterest. The primary scalable growth lever for most DTC brands.
How to say it "Paid social is where we operate — Meta and TikTok specifically. These platforms let us target the right person with the right message at the right time."
Media Buying
The process of purchasing ad placements across platforms — deciding where, when, and how much to spend to hit campaign goals efficiently.
How to say it "Our media buying is performance-focused — we're not just placing ads, we're constantly analyzing data and reallocating spend to what's working."
Creative Strategy
The plan behind what ads to make — what hooks to test, what angles to run, what messages to match to which audiences.
How to say it "Creative strategy is where we spend most of our brainpower — because in today's landscape, creative IS the targeting. Bad creative means wasted spend no matter how good the setup is."
